Mr. Hwang is currently doing his undergraduate degree in Electronics Engineering at Kyungpook National University, Daegu, Republic of Korea. His research interests include the development of Error-Correcting Codes (ECC) to detect and correct errors that may occur during data transmission or storage, with applications in fields such as automotive, aerospace, and military sectors.

Research Topic

Error Correction Code (ECC) for Robust Processor Architecture

In on-chip communication, There is a possibility of bit corruption during the transmission or storage of data and signals. This issue is especially serious in semiconductors used in extreme environments like vehicle, aerospace, and military applications, where electro-magnetic and physical shocks are frequent. Additionally, as chip sizes have been shrinking recently, they have become increasingly sensitive to noise. In such environments, the likelihood of errors during data storage and transmission is higher. For devices that require high reliability, such data errors can lead to serious consequences, making it important to control these errors.

To resolve this issue, Error-Correcting Codes (ECC) can be used to protect data from corruption and ensure data integrity and reliability. In the process of data transmission, ECC is generated by adding parity bits, which are created by combining the original data data bits. When receiving data, ECC checks the original data along with the parity bits to find the location of any error and correct it by flipping the affected bit. By implementing ECC modules at the hardware level within the system-on-chip bus, the stability and reliability of the system can be substantially enhanced.
